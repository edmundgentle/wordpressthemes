The Alarm Without A Snooze Button

Edmund Gentle
Arts Editor
7th October 2013
  • Style & Beauty
  • Features
  • News
  • Arts & Entertainment

You’re snuggled in your bed, all warm and cosy. Not a care in the world! You’re a Fresher and you just had the most incredible night. That person totally checked you out … you downed your Terminator faster than all your mates and you even started the first round of “Whoop, whoop!” to that song that you really can’t stand. Your entire mind is buzzing with an electrifying brilliance until . . .

Once upon a time there was a guy called Jim who lived on a farm and he liked living on a farm because it was very farmy

Satan decides to call upon you in the form of the most annoying, ear-shattering siren you have ever had the misfortune to hear.

Once the bane of everyone’s lives begins to sound, you can hear the sudden explosion of hundreds of students’ brains. Those suffering with the nights aftermath, sob their poor, drunken little hearts out. Those who have early lectures begin to boil, and sometimes, if you look really closely . . . you can see murder behind their eyes.

“In my three weeks of living here, the fire alarm has gone off more times than I can count”

Then, of course, you have to worry about whether your pyjamas (or lack of) are suitable for The March. (As a female, I sometimes find the four minutes a little mean.) Do the ladies cover up with a hoody or a cute fluffy gown? Do the lads put on some pants and boldly make fun of the situation or, do they also dress in a cute fluffy dressing gown?? Then of course, there are a few who don’t mind risking the fine and simply sleep through The Devils call. We salute you.

“In my three weeks of living here, the fire alarm has gone off more times than I can count”

Once changed, the thunderous rush of hundreds of vexed students begin roar down the staircase. Until you hit the front door.

Oh good, no rain. It’s quite pleasant. Perfect weather conditions for a leisurely stroll, if it wasn’t for the fact that it’s three in the morning; I’m tired, angry, confused and I was in the middle of an incredible dream.

Don’t even get me started on the gruelling twenty-minute wait, where we are all huddled together like pissed off penguins, our eyes angrily darting around in an effort to spot the perpetrator. Then, all the RA’s begin to conspire with each other at the front of the group, as they wait for their Almighty Leader, who leisurely takes his time to join us.

Oh, wonderful. The culprit wasn’t even a resident of our Halls. Well, thank you very much to the person who allowed that to enter. No, really. Well done. A round of applause for you, you massive turd.

Ten minutes or so later, your head hits your glorious pillow. Relief; sweet, blessed relief.

In my three weeks of living here, the fire alarm has gone off more times than I can count (I’m really bad at math). I hate it more than I hate my neighbours back home, or the football. Think of the most aggravating person you know and times their personality by a million.

They still won’t compare to the fire alarm.
